Hosting & Deployment Guide

Web Hosting vs. Deployment

Web Hosting: Provides the infrastructure (servers, storage, etc.) to store and display your website.

Deployment: The process of making your website live and accessible to users.

Key Steps in Website Hosting

  • Choose a Hosting Provider: Research different providers and select one that meets your needs (e.g., shared, VPS, dedicated hosting).
  • Select a Hosting Plan: Choose a plan that offers the resources (storage, bandwidth, etc.) you need.
  • Register a Domain Name: Purchase a domain name that represents your website.
  • Set up Email Accounts: Configure email addresses associated with your domain.

Key Steps in Website Deployment

  • Preparation: Ensure your website files are organized and ready for deployment.
  • Set up DNS Records: Configure your domain's DNS records to point to your hosting server.
  • Test your Website: Set up a live testing site to ensure everything works before going live.
  • Backup and Go Live: Back up your website files and then make your website accessible to users.
  • Choose a Deployment Method: Consider manual deployment (using FTP/SSH) or using a deployment tool.
  • Connect Your Repository: If using a deployment tool, connect it to your version control repository.
  • Deploy Your Website: Follow the deployment tool's instructions or use FTP/SSH to upload your files.

Additional Tips

  • Version Control: Use a version control system (e.g., Git) to track changes and facilitate deployments.
  • Testing: Thoroughly test your website before and after deployment.
  • Monitoring: Implement monitoring tools to track website performance and identify potential issues.
  • Documentation: Create clear documentation for your deployment process.
